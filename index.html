Raw Materials

The Components
Behind The Art

Collected from donation drives, IT recycling centers, and tech departments — these are the building blocks of TechWall.

🖥 CPU CHIPS Intel & AMD processors of all generations form dense mosaic tiles
⌨️ KEYBOARD KEYS Individual keycaps sorted by color create pixel-art lettering
💾 HDD PLATTERS Reflective hard drive discs used as accent pieces and backgrounds
🐭 MOUSE PARTS Scroll wheels, sensors and buttons for fine detail work
🔌 CIRCUIT BOARDS PCB segments with etched copper traces form intricate patterns
🔋 RAM STICKS Memory modules act as structural bars and framing elements
Process

From Collection
To Creation

01
Phase 01 — Collection
E-Waste Gathering Drive
Community drop-offs, IT department donations, and recycling centers contribute over 3 months.
02
Phase 02 — Sorting
Categorization & Prep
Components sorted by type, color, and size. Cleaned and catalogued for use.
03
Phase 03 — Design
Digital Blueprint
Each tech icon is digitally mapped to specific components. Pixel-by-pixel planning.
04
Phase 04 — Construction
Assembly & Mounting
Components mounted onto reinforced wall panels section by section.
05
Phase 05 — Launch
Unveiling & Exhibition
Public unveiling with guided tours, QR experiences, and e-waste workshops.
Why It Matters

The Impact

TechWall is more than art — it's a statement about responsible technology and the circular economy.

100+
Kilograms Diverted

Every kilo of e-waste diverted from landfill prevents toxic chemicals like lead and mercury from entering soil and water.

10K+
Expected Visitors

The installation will educate thousands of students, professionals and community members about e-waste and the IT ecosystem.

40+
Technologies Celebrated

From assembly language to cloud platforms, TechWall documents the full spectrum of technologies powering our digital civilization.
