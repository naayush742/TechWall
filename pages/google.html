History

Google was founded on September 4, 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin as a Stanford University PhD project. The name derives from 'googol' (10^100), reflecting the mission to organize vast information. The PageRank algorithm, which ranked pages by the number and quality of links pointing to them, was revolutionary. Google went public in August 2004. Alphabet Inc. was created as Google's parent company in 2015. Google now processes over 8.5 billion searches per day.

Visual Overview

Why We Use Google

Google's search algorithms consider hundreds of factors including content quality, page speed, mobile-friendliness, backlinks, and increasingly AI-powered relevance signals (MUM, BERT, RankBrain). For developers and businesses, understanding Google's ecosystem — Search Console, Ads, Analytics, Cloud, and APIs — is essential.

Applications & Use Cases

  • Web search (8.5 billion daily queries)
  • Google Ads (pay-per-click digital advertising)
  • Google Analytics (website traffic analysis)
  • Google Maps and navigation
  • YouTube (video hosting, owned by Google)
  • Google Cloud Platform (compute, storage, AI)

Key Facts & Features

  • Google holds ~92% global search market share
  • Core Web Vitals measure user experience signals for SEO
  • BERT (2019) and MUM (2021): AI models improving search understanding
  • Knowledge Graph contains 500+ billion facts about real-world entities
  • Google Search Console is free for webmasters to monitor site performance
  • Google processes 2+ trillion searches per year globally
