History

🕰️

OneDrive was launched by Microsoft in August 2007 under the name 'Windows Live SkyDrive'. It was rebranded to OneDrive in January 2014 after a trademark dispute with British Sky Broadcasting. Initially offering 5 GB of free storage, it has grown into a deeply integrated component of Microsoft 365 and Windows 11, serving hundreds of millions of users worldwide.

Why We Use OneDrive

OneDrive offers seamless integration with Windows and Microsoft 365 apps, automatic photo backup from mobile devices, real-time collaboration on Word, Excel, and PowerPoint files, advanced sharing controls, and enterprise-grade security. It eliminates the need for physical storage and ensures files are accessible from any device anywhere.
