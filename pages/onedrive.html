01 — Overview

What exactly is OneDrive?

A cloud-based home for your files that's woven directly into Windows and Microsoft 365 — so saving a Word document already means it's backed up, synced, and shareable.

  • ☁️
    Cloud storage

    Files live on Microsoft's servers and stay accessible from any signed-in device, anywhere.

  • 🔄
    Files On-Demand

    See every file in File Explorer without downloading it first — only the files you open take up local space.

  • 📝
    Real-time co-authoring

    Multiple people can edit the same Word, Excel, or PowerPoint file at once, directly in the desktop or web app.

  • 🔐
    Personal Vault

    An extra-secure folder protected by two-step verification, ideal for IDs, passports, and financial documents.

02 — History

From a quiet beta to the backbone of Windows

OneDrive went through more rebrands than almost any other major cloud product — partly by design, and once because of a lawsuit.

Aug 2007

Quiet launch as "Windows Live Folders"

Microsoft starts a limited beta in the US under the internal codename SkyDrive, before renaming it Windows Live SkyDrive just over a week later and opening it to testers in the UK and India.

2008

Global expansion & more storage

SkyDrive reaches 38 countries by May, expanding to 62 soon after. Individual storage jumps from 5 GB to 25 GB, and Windows Live Photos adds photo-specific browsing.

2010

Office Web Apps integration

The "Wave 4" update lets users save Office documents straight to SkyDrive from the browser and view version history — the first real bridge to today's Office + OneDrive workflow.

2013

The Sky UK lawsuit

British broadcaster Sky UK wins a trademark case arguing "SkyDrive" infringes on its "Sky" brand — forcing Microsoft to find a new name.

Feb 2014

SkyDrive officially becomes OneDrive

The rename rolls out across most platforms on February 19, 2014, retiring the SkyDrive name for good.

2015–17

New web design & Files On-Demand

A redesigned OneDrive for the web ships in 2015, and Files On-Demand launches in 2017 — letting every cloud file appear in File Explorer without eating local disk space.

2018

Known Folder Move

Desktop, Documents, and Pictures folders can now back up to OneDrive automatically, with zero manual setup — quietly protecting millions of PCs from data loss.

2024–26

The Copilot era

Microsoft 365 plans fold in Copilot AI features alongside OneDrive storage — image generation, writing help, and ransomware protection now ship as one bundled subscription.

03 — Use cases

Who actually relies on OneDrive

OneDrive's biggest strength is invisibility — it works quietly in the background of tools people already use every day.

Office workers

Word, Excel & PowerPoint files

Open a Word doc on your laptop, keep editing on your phone — autosave and version history mean nothing is ever truly lost.

Business

OneDrive for Business & SharePoint

Teams get pooled storage, admin controls, and compliance tools — the same engine that powers SharePoint behind the scenes.

PC backup

Known Folder Move

Desktop, Documents, and Pictures back up automatically, so a stolen or broken laptop doesn't mean losing your files.

Sensitive files

Personal Vault

An identity-verified, extra-locked folder for passports, IDs, and financial paperwork — separate from everyday files.

Xbox & gaming

Cross-console saves & settings

OneDrive syncs settings and saves across Xbox consoles and Windows, alongside its original role storing documents and photos.

Families

Microsoft 365 Family sharing

Up to six people each get their own 1 TB OneDrive plus the full Office app suite, under one shared subscription.

06 — How it works

What happens when you save a file

Files On-Demand makes OneDrive feel local even though most of it lives in the cloud.

STEP 1

You save in File Explorer or Office

A file changes in your OneDrive folder, or directly inside Word, Excel, or PowerPoint.

STEP 2

The client flags the change

The OneDrive sync engine, built into Windows, detects the new or edited file right away.

STEP 3

Upload to Microsoft's servers

Only the changed bytes are sent, encrypted in transit, and written to Microsoft's cloud storage.

STEP 4

Synced everywhere instantly

Every other device you're signed into — and anyone the file is shared with — sees the change appear.

07 — Good to know

OneDrive vs. Google Drive, in short

Both do the same core job — cloud storage and sync — but lean toward different ecosystems.

OneDrive wins on

  • Deep Windows & File Explorer integration
  • Real-time co-authoring inside desktop Office apps
  • Bundled value if you already pay for Microsoft 365
  • Personal Vault's extra identity-verified security layer

Google Drive wins on

  • A larger free tier (15 GB vs. 5 GB)
  • Browser-first collaboration with Docs, Sheets, Slides
  • Cross-platform feel that doesn't favor one OS
  • Simpler personal pricing tiers via Google One
