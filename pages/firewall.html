History

🕰️

The concept of firewalls in networking emerged in the late 1980s. The first paper on firewall technology was published by Jeff Mogul at DEC in 1988. The first commercial firewall products were released around 1991 by DEC and Check Point. Stateful inspection was introduced by Check Point in 1994, revolutionizing how firewalls track connection states. Next-Generation Firewalls (NGFW) emerged in the 2000s, adding deep packet inspection, IDS/IPS, and application-layer awareness.

Why We Use Firewall

Firewalls are the first line of defense in network security. They enforce security policies by allowing legitimate traffic while blocking unauthorized access, malware communication, and intrusion attempts. Modern NGFWs go beyond simple port/protocol filtering to inspect application content, detect anomalies, and integrate with threat intelligence feeds.

Key Facts & Features

  • 🧠 Packet filtering
    allows/blocks by IP, port, protocol
  • 🎯 Stateful inspection
    tracks active connections and their state
  • ⚡ Deep Packet Inspection (DPI)
    reads application-layer data
  • ⚠️ Popular software firewalls
    iptables/nftables, pfSense, UFW, Windows Defender Firewall
  • 📌 Next-Gen vendors
    Palo Alto, Fortinet, Cisco ASA, Check Point
  • 💡 WAF providers
    Cloudflare, AWS WAF, Imperva
