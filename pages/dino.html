CHROME EASTER EGG

T-REX RUNNER: THE DINO GAME

The most famous Easter egg in software history, turning frustrating downtime into a pixelated adventure.

Officially known as Chrome Dino, this game is a symbolic nod to the "prehistoric ages" before WiFi, played over 270 million times every month.

Origin Story

The Bolan Legacy

Born in 2014, the Chrome Dino Game was originally codenamed "Project Bolan" as a tribute to Marc Bolan, the lead singer of the 1970s rock band T. Rex. Created by Google's design team (Sebastien Gabriel, Alan Bettes, and Edward Jung), it was intended to humanize the frustration of a lost internet connection.

The pixelated aesthetic was a deliberate choice to evoke the "prehistoric ages" before the ubiquity of high-speed WiFi, turning a technical failure into a moment of nostalgic play.

Gameplay Mechanics

Minimalism as a Mastery

The core purpose of the Dino Runner is to provide an infinite, addictive challenge using just two keys: space to jump and the down arrow to duck. As the dinosaur sprints across the desert, the speed increases linearly, requiring frame-perfect reflexes to avoid cacti and pterodactyls.

Technically, the game is designed to be "unbeatable"—it is programmed to last for approximately **17 million years**, mirroring the actual time the Tyrannosaurus Rex inhabited the Earth.

A Global Cultural Landmark

What started as a hidden Easter egg has become a global phenomenon. Played over **270 million times every month**, the Dino Game is a symbolic bridge between our high-tech present and the silent, offline past. It represents the ultimate success of UX design: turning a user's worst-case scenario (no internet) into their favorite hidden feature.
