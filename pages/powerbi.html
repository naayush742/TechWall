01 — Overview

What exactly is Power BI?

Power BI connects to dozens of data sources, lets you clean and model that data, then turns it into interactive charts, tables, and dashboards — all without writing code.

  • 🔌
    Power Query

    Connect to 100+ data sources and clean, reshape, and merge data with a no-code interface.

  • 🧮
    Data modeling & DAX

    Build relationships between tables and write formulas in DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) for custom calculations.

  • 📊
    Interactive visuals

    Click a bar in one chart and every other visual on the page filters instantly to match — no extra code needed.

  • ☁️
    Power BI Service

    Publish reports to the cloud, schedule automatic data refreshes, and share live dashboards across a team.

02 — History

From three separate Excel add-ins to a unified platform

Power BI wasn't designed as one product — it was assembled from tools that had been quietly proving themselves inside Excel for years.

2009–10

"Project Gemini" becomes Power Pivot

Microsoft renames its internal "Gemini" project to Power Pivot and ships it as a free Excel add-in, introducing the DAX formula language and a fast in-memory engine (VertiPaq) for analyzing millions of rows.

2012

Power View arrives

First released in SharePoint, Power View brings drag-and-drop, click-to-filter interactive visuals — laying the groundwork for today's report canvas.

2013

Power Query & "Power BI for Office 365"

"Data Explorer" is renamed Power Query for cleaning messy data, and Microsoft bundles all three add-ins into the first product to carry the Power BI name — though it still depends entirely on Excel and SharePoint.

Jul 2015

Power BI launches standalone

On July 24, 2015, Microsoft rebuilds everything into Power BI Desktop, a free standalone app, paired with the cloud-based Power BI Service — finally free from needing Excel to function.

2016–19

Explosive growth & market leadership

Monthly feature updates and tight Microsoft 365 integration drive rapid adoption, and Power BI climbs to the top of Gartner's Magic Quadrant for Analytics and BI Platforms.

2020–22

AI-powered insights

Azure AI integration adds Smart Narratives, automated anomaly detection, and the "Key Influencers" visual — surfacing insights without manual digging.

2023–26

Microsoft Fabric & Copilot

Power BI becomes part of Microsoft Fabric, a unified analytics platform, while Copilot lets users ask plain-English questions and get auto-generated reports in response.

03 — Use cases

Who actually builds dashboards in Power BI

From a single analyst tracking sales to an entire enterprise standardizing its reporting, the same tool scales across roles.

Sales & marketing

Pipeline & campaign dashboards

Live dashboards track deal stages, conversion rates, and ad spend — refreshed automatically instead of rebuilt by hand each week.

Finance

Budget & forecasting reports

Finance teams connect directly to ERP systems to build budget-vs-actual reports that update the moment new numbers land.

Operations

Supply chain & inventory tracking

Real-time visibility into stock levels, delivery times, and bottlenecks across an entire supply chain in one report.

Healthcare

Patient & resource analytics

Hospitals track bed occupancy, wait times, and outcomes data to manage capacity and improve patient care.

Students & learners

Portfolio & coursework projects

Power BI's free Desktop app lets students practice real data analysis and build a visible portfolio without paying anything.

Executives

Company-wide KPI dashboards

Leadership teams get a single live view across every department instead of waiting on a monthly slide deck.

04 — How it works

From raw data to a shared dashboard

Every Power BI project — small or enterprise-scale — follows roughly the same four-step loop.

STEP 1

Get data

Connect to Excel, SQL Server, SharePoint, Salesforce, or 100+ other sources using Power Query.

STEP 2

Clean & model

Remove errors, merge tables, define relationships, and write DAX measures for custom calculations.

STEP 3

Build the report

Drag charts, maps, and tables onto the canvas — every visual cross-filters the others automatically.

STEP 4

Publish & refresh

Push the report to Power BI Service, schedule automatic data refreshes, and share it as a live dashboard.

05 — Pricing

Free to build, paid to share at scale

Power BI Desktop is free forever — payment enters the picture once you publish and share reports with a team.

Desktop
$0
Free forever. Build reports and explore data locally — the personal authoring tool used by every Power BI user.
Pro
$14/user/mo
The standard tier for publishing to shared workspaces, collaborating, and consuming reports others have shared.
Premium Per User
$24/user/mo
Adds paginated reports, larger datasets, 48 daily refreshes, and advanced AI features for power users.
Fabric Capacity
$262/mo+
Organization-level capacity (from the F2 tier) that lets unlimited free-tier viewers access shared reports.

Power BI Pro is included at no extra cost with Microsoft 365 E5 and Office 365 E5 subscriptions. Pricing shown reflects 2026 list rates and changes over time — check Microsoft's official pricing page for current numbers.
