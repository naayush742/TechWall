History

🕰️

FORTRAN (FORmula TRANslation) was developed by John Backus and his team at IBM between 1954 and 1957. It was the first widely used high-level programming language and the first to have an optimizing compiler. The idea — that a compiler could produce code as efficient as hand-written assembly — was considered impossible by many until Backus proved them wrong. Fortran 90 (1991) introduced array operations, modules, and recursion. Modern Fortran (2018) is still actively updated.

Why We Use Fortran

Fortran was designed for numerical computation and scientific calculation, and decades of optimization have made it extraordinarily fast for floating-point operations. Many fundamental numerical libraries (BLAS, LAPACK) were written in Fortran and remain the gold standard for linear algebra. Climate models, computational fluid dynamics, and quantum chemistry codes still run in Fortran.

    A = B + C works element-wise

    foundational linear algebra libraries written in Fortran
