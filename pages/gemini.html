Built multimodal from day one — not bolted on afterward.

Gemini is Google's family of large language models, trained natively on text, images, audio, video, and code simultaneously rather than separately. This page covers where it came from, how that "natively multimodal" design actually works, where it's used today, and where to learn it properly.

01 — Origin

Two AI labs merge, and a ChatGPT rival is born

Gemini's origin story is really the story of two competing Google research groups joining forces under outside pressure.

Google's first public answer to ChatGPT was Bard, announced on February 6, 2023, and initially powered by Google's existing LaMDA language model. Bard rolled out to a small group of trusted testers before a wider release later that month. Around the same time, Google merged its two major AI research divisions — Google Brain and DeepMind — into a single unit called Google DeepMind, setting up the team that would build Gemini.

Gemini itself was first announced at the Google I/O keynote on May 10, 2023, positioned as a more powerful successor to PaLM 2. Unlike most LLMs of the era — which were trained on text and then had vision or audio capabilities added afterward — Gemini was designed to be natively multimodal from the start, trained simultaneously on text, images, audio, video, and code. Google co-founder Sergey Brin came out of retirement to contribute to the project alongside hundreds of engineers.

On December 6, 2023, Google CEO Sundar Pichai and DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis formally launched Gemini 1.0 in three sizes — Ultra (complex tasks), Pro (general use, which immediately began powering Bard), and Nano (on-device, launching on the Pixel 8 Pro). In February 2024, Google merged Bard and its Duet AI assistant under a single new brand: Gemini. The same month brought Gemini 1.5, with a then-massive one-million-token context window capable of processing roughly an hour of video in a single prompt. The pace has only accelerated since — Gemini 2.0 arrived in December 2024 with native real-time audio/video and agentic capabilities, followed by Gemini 2.5 in 2025 emphasizing step-by-step "thinking," and Gemini 3 in late 2025/2026, which broke 1500 Elo on the LMArena leaderboard.

02 — Mechanics

What "natively multimodal" actually means

Most earlier AI systems bolted vision or audio onto a text-only model after the fact. Gemini was trained on all of these data types together, from the very first training run.

Because every modality shares one learned representation space, Gemini can reason across them at once — e.g. watching a video, listening to its audio, and reading comments about it together, rather than handling each separately.

◆ Multimodal

Capable of understanding and generating across text, images, audio, video, and code within the same model.

◆ Context window

How much information the model can "hold in mind" at once — Gemini's million-plus token window can span an entire codebase or hours of video.

◆ Mixture-of-experts (MoE)

An architecture that activates only the relevant sub-sections of the model per query, improving speed and efficiency.

◆ Gems

Custom, reusable chatbot personas users can configure within Gemini for specific recurring tasks or roles.

◆ Gemini Live

A real-time voice conversation mode that lets users talk to Gemini naturally, with live audio and video understanding.

◆ Gemma

A smaller, free, open-source sibling model family, released separately from the proprietary Gemini line.

03 — Applications

Where Gemini shows up across Google's products

Rather than living only in a standalone chatbot, Gemini is woven directly into the tools hundreds of millions of people already use daily.

Usecase 01

The Gemini app & web chatbot

A direct ChatGPT-style conversational assistant available via gemini.google.com and native mobile/desktop apps.

Usecase 02

Google Workspace

Integrated into Gmail, Docs, Sheets, and Slides to draft emails, summarize threads, and generate content directly inside the apps.

Usecase 03

Android & Pixel devices

Gemini Nano runs on-device for fast, private features like Smart Reply and audio summarization, without needing a server round-trip.

Usecase 04

Google Search & AI Mode

Powers AI-generated overviews and conversational search experiences layered on top of traditional search results.

Usecase 05

Software development

Available via Google AI Studio and Vertex AI for building applications, plus agentic coding tools for end-to-end development tasks.

Usecase 06

Creative generation

Powers image generation (via Imagen) and short music/video generation features directly inside the Gemini app.
