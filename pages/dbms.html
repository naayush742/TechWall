Database Management and Business Intelligence

This page explores the foundational technologies for storing, retrieving, and visualizing data in the modern enterprise.

Microsoft Power BI

Originally conceived as "Project Crescent" in 2011, Power BI is a powerful business analytics service by Microsoft. It provides interactive visualizations and business intelligence capabilities with an interface simple enough for end users to create their own reports and dashboards.

Power BI excels at connecting to hundreds of data sources, simplifying data prep, and driving ad hoc analysis. It has become a leader in the BI space, empowering organizations to make data-driven decisions through beautiful, real-time insights.

MySQL

Launched in May 1995 by Michael "Monty" Widenius and David Axmark, MySQL is the world's most popular open-source relational database management system (RDBMS). It is a central component of the LAMP stack (Linux, Apache, MySQL, PHP/Python/Perl).

Known for its reliability, ease of use, and performance, MySQL powers many of the world's most frequently visited websites, including Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube. Despite its acquisition by Oracle, it remains a cornerstone of the web's data infrastructure.

MongoDB

Developed in 2007 by 10gen (now MongoDB Inc.), MongoDB led the NoSQL revolution. Instead of traditional rows and columns, MongoDB stores data in flexible, JSON-like documents, making it ideal for applications with evolving schemas and rapid growth.

With its horizontal scaling capabilities through sharding and its native support for high availability, MongoDB has become the database of choice for modern cloud-native applications, microservices, and real-time data processing.
