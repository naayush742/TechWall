History

🕰️

Bug bounty programs have roots in Netscape's 1995 program offering $1,000 for Netscape Navigator 2.0 bugs. The modern era began when Mozilla launched a formal program in 2004. HackerOne and Bugcrowd, the two largest bug bounty platforms, were both founded in 2012. In 2014 the US Department of Defense launched 'Hack the Pentagon' — the first federal bug bounty. Google, Apple, Microsoft, and Meta all run programs paying millions annually.

Why We Use Bug Bounty

Bug bounty programs create a legal and financial framework for ethical hackers to find and report vulnerabilities before malicious actors do. They are cost-effective for organizations (only pay for valid findings) and provide career income and recognition for security researchers. Platforms like HackerOne have paid over $300 million to hackers cumulatively.
