The Journey

Genesis of the Unlicenced Spectrum

The wireless revolution was born from a 1985 regulatory decision to release the ISM (Industrial, Scientific, and Medical) bands for unlicenced use. By 1999, the "Wi-Fi" brand was created to provide a consumer-friendly identity for the **IEEE 802.11b** protocol. What began as an experimental way to connect cash registers transformed into the invisible infrastructure of the modern world.

From the modest 11 Mbps of the first generation to the multi-gigabit horizons of today, WiFi has evolved through a relentless pursuit of speed, range, and congestion management.

Standards & Security

The Architecture of Invisible Waves

The mission of modern networking is to provide "invisible wiring" for the global economy. This is achieved through the architectural complexity of **WiFi 6E and WiFi 7**, which utilize the 6 GHz band and **Extremely High Throughput (EHT)** technologies to deliver fiber-like speeds without the physical cables.

Parallel to speed, security has matured from the flawed WEP to the robust **WPA3** encryption. By untethering us from the physical desk, WiFi has fundamentally enabled the explosion of mobile computing, remote work, and the Internet of Things (IoT).
